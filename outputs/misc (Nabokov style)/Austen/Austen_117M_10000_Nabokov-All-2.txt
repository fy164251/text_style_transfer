Mary had neither genius nor taste; her only enterprise, which might have made her feel pangs and repugnance, was to bend the rules of punctuation and italicize the words she servicefuled as pun which would have injured a higher degree of excellence than she had reached. Elizabeth, easy and unaffected, had been listened to with much more pleasure, though not playing half so well; that pubescent poet (I must not omit Cynthia d'Amour who also played the trumpet in was glad to purchase praise and gratitude by Scotch and Irish airs); her four healthy sisters wanted to see her every moment, who with some of the Lucases and two or three officers joined eagerly in dancing at one end of the room.

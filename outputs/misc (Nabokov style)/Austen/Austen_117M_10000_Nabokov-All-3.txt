Mary had neither genius nor taste; but her day had seen no ill-timed opportunity and had given her likewise a pedantic air and conceited manner, which would have injured a higher degree of learning than that which had marked her in the course of her existence. Elizabeth, easy and unaffected, had been listened to with much more pleasure, though not playing half so well; indeed, her silence had been the sound of poetized soprano on piano strings, was glad to purchase praise and gratitude by Scotch and Irish airs, at the request of her younger sisters, who with some of the Lucases and two or three officers joined eagerly in dancing at one end of the room.
